## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

### Review title
Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Botulinum Toxin Type A and Hyaluronic Acid Combination Therapy for Facial Rejuvenation: Assessing Clinical Efficacy, Patient Satisfaction, and Safety Outcomes

### Review objectives
What are the comparative clinical efficacy, patient satisfaction, and safety outcomes for patients undergoing botulinum toxin type A and hyaluronic acid combination therapy against those undergoing botulinum monotherapy?

**Population (P):** Patients undergoing facial rejuvenation procedures. Typically includes adults (likely middle-aged to older adults) seeking treatment for facial wrinkles, volume loss, and other signs of aging.

**Intervention (I):** Combination therapy of Botulinum Toxin Type A (e.g., Botox) and Hyaluronic Acid (dermal fillers).

**Comparison (C):** Botulinum Toxin Type A monotherapy (Botox alone) without the addition of Hyaluronic Acid.

**Outcomes (O):** Clinical Efficacy: Measured by the reduction in wrinkles, improved skin appearance, or other relevant clinical outcomes. Patient Satisfaction: Assessed via patient-reported outcomes, surveys, or satisfaction scales. Safety Outcomes: Incidence of adverse events, side effects, or complications associated with the treatments.

**Study Design (S):** Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of randomized controlled trials (RCTs), observational studies, or cohort studies comparing the above interventions.

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

### Main outcomes

**Clinical Efficacy**
- Measurement: Validated aesthetic scales (WSRS or GAIS)
- Timing: Baseline, 4 weeks, 3 months, 6 months post-treatment

**Patient Satisfaction**
- Measurement: Patient-reported outcome measures (PROMs), satisfaction questionnaires, or VAS
- Timing: 4 weeks, 3 months, 6 months post-procedure

**Safety Outcomes**
- Measurement: Number and type of adverse events (injection site reactions, bruising, swelling, asymmetry, allergic reactions)

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

### Data extraction (selection and coding)

**Inclusion Criteria:**
- Adults undergoing facial rejuvenation treatments using botulinum toxin type A and/or hyaluronic acid
- Studies investigating combination therapy and/or monotherapy
- Comparative studies between combination therapy and monotherapy
- Studies reporting efficacy, satisfaction, or safety outcomes

**Search Strategy:** PubMed, Embase, and Cochrane Library using keywords including "botulinum toxin type A," "hyaluronic acid," "facial rejuvenation," "combination therapy," "patient satisfaction," "safety outcomes" with Boolean operators.

**Screening Process:**
- Initial screening: Two independent reviewers screen titles/abstracts
- Full-text review: Same reviewers assess full texts
- Final selection: Studies meeting all inclusion criteria

**Data Extraction:**
- Study characteristics: authors, publication year, study design, sample size
- Participant demographics: age, gender, baseline skin condition
- Intervention details: dosage, frequency, technique of administration
- Two researchers independently extract data; discrepancies resolved through discussion

### Risk of bias (quality) assessment
- Formal risk of bias assessment tool employed
- For randomized trials: Cochrane Risk of Bias tool (selection, performance, detection, attrition, reporting bias)
- Study design, participant characteristics, and outcome measures evaluated

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

### Strategy for data synthesis

**Systematic Review:**
- Comprehensive narrative synthesis
- Findings categorized by primary outcomes
- Patterns, similarities, differences across studies identified

**Meta-Analysis:**
- Performed if studies sufficiently homogeneous
- Continuous outcomes: mean differences or SMDs
- Categorical outcomes: odds ratios (ORs) or risk ratios (RRs)
- Subgroup analyses by treatment type, patient age, follow-up duration

**Meta-Analysis Models and Statistical Heterogeneity:**
- Continuous outcomes: random-effects models (if heterogeneity anticipated); fixed-effects (if homogeneous)
- Categorical outcomes: Mantel-Haenszel (fixed-effects); DerSimonian-Laird (random-effects)
- Heterogeneity: I² statistic (25% low, 50% moderate, 75% high)
- If I² > 50%: explore sources via subgroup analyses or meta-regression
- Sensitivity analyses to assess impact of individual studies

### Analysis of subgroups or subsets
- Type of facial rejuvenation approach (simultaneous vs. sequential injections)
- Baseline skin condition (Fitzpatrick skin types, wrinkle severity)
- Random-effects model for each subgroup
- Sensitivity analyses within each subgroup
