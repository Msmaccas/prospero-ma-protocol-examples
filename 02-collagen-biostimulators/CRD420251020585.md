## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

### Review title
Efficacy and Outcomes of Collagen Biostimulator Injections for Facial Aesthetics: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

### Condition or domain being studied
Well Adult; Healthy

This systematic review focuses on the use of injectable collagen biostimulators in healthy adult populations for facial aesthetic improvement. The review will assess aesthetic outcomes, primarily Global Aesthetic Improvement Score (GAIS), following treatment with biostimulators such as PLLA, CaHA, PDO, PDLLA, and PCL.

### Rationale for the review
Collagen biostimulators have become increasingly popular in facial aesthetics due to their ability to induce natural collagen production. Despite their widespread use, comparative data on their clinical efficacy remains limited. This systematic review aims to synthesize available evidence on the effectiveness of collagen biostimulator injections, with GAIS as the primary outcome measure.

### Review objectives
To systematically evaluate and compare the aesthetic outcomes of collagen biostimulator injections for facial rejuvenation using the Global Aesthetic Improvement Score (GAIS) as the primary outcome measure.

### Keywords
Collagen biostimulator; Facial rejuvenation; Global aesthetic improvement score; Systematic review

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

### Population
- **Included:** Healthy adults (≥18 years) receiving collagen biostimulator injections for facial aesthetics
- **Excluded:** Participants <18 years, non-human studies, or treatments for non-aesthetic purposes

### Intervention(s) or exposure(s)
Studies that involve collagen biostimulator injections for facial aesthetic purposes, including but not limited to poly-L-lactic acid (PLLA), calcium hydroxylapatite (CaHA), polydioxanone (PDO), poly-D,L-lactic acid (PDLLA), and polycaprolactone (PCL). All types of injection techniques and treatment regimens are eligible.

### Comparator(s) or control(s)
Placebo; Others. This review will include studies comparing collagen biostimulator injections with placebo, no treatment, or other aesthetic interventions.

### Study design
Both randomized and nonrandomized study types will be included. Specifically: randomized controlled trials, prospective cohort studies, and comparative clinical trials evaluating synthetic collagen biostimulators for facial aesthetic purposes.

### Context
Studies conducted in clinical or aesthetic medical settings assessing synthetic collagen biostimulators for facial rejuvenation in healthy adult populations, regardless of country or healthcare system.

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

### Main outcomes
**Primary outcome:** Global Aesthetic Improvement Scale (GAIS), as assessed by either physicians or patients. All time points reported for GAIS will be included.

### Additional outcomes
Secondary outcomes: Other reported outcomes related to aesthetic improvement, patient satisfaction, or treatment durability may also be collected if available.

---

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

### Strategy for data synthesis
Data will be synthesized using a **random-effects meta-analysis model**, assuming clinical and methodological heterogeneity across studies.
- Continuous outcomes (GAIS): standardized mean differences (SMD) with 95% CI
- Dichotomous outcomes (adverse events): risk ratios (RR)
- Heterogeneity: I² statistic
- Subgroup analyses: types of biostimulators and study design (RCT vs non-RCT)
- Publication bias: funnel plots and Egger's test (if >10 studies available)
