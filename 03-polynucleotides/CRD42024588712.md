## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

### Review title
The effectiveness of polynucleotides in aesthetics medicine/ facial aesthetics : a systematic review

### Review objectives
1) What is the application of polynucleotides in aesthetic medicine?
2) What is the effectiveness of polynucleotides in aesthetic medicine?

### Keywords
Polynucleotides / Aesthetic Medicine

---

## SEARCHING AND SCREENING

### Searches
A systematic review of Embase, MEDLINE, Cochrane Library and ClinicalTrials.gov will be performed according to PRISMA guidelines. Boolean operators will combine search concepts and subject headings (e.g. "AND"; "OR").

Studies will be included if published between **2010 to the date of the search**. **No language restrictions** will be applied at the study selection stage.

Search terms include: "polynucleotide", "polydeoxyribonucleotide", "aesthetic medicine" and "facial aesthetic".

### Study design
All primary studies assessing the use of PN in aesthetic medicine published in the last decade will be included.

**Excluded:** Systematic reviews, meta-analyses, case reports, theses, conference abstracts, and prospective studies without results.

**Included study types:**
- Randomised controlled trials (including cluster RCTs)
- Non-randomised controlled trials
- Cluster-randomised trials, cluster non-randomised trials
- Observational studies (cohort, prospective/retrospective comparative cohort, case-control, cross-sectional, longitudinal)
- Quasi-experimental studies (quasi-randomised trials, CBA, Q-ITS)

---

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

### Condition or domain being studied
The use of polynucleotides (PN) in aesthetic medicine. Studies for more than one skin/hair rejuvenation treatments, including PN, will also be considered if PN data can be isolated.

### Population
Patients undergoing PN treatment for facial rejuvenation. Animal studies or laboratory experiments will be excluded.

**Exclusion criteria:** Pregnancy or breastfeeding, patients who have undergone alternative rejuvenation treatments performed less than six months before enrolment, and previous cosmetic surgery.

### Intervention(s) or exposure(s)
Any intervention with the primary effect of facial rejuvenation post PN. Studies may target one facial area (e.g., crow's feet) or multiple areas (e.g., whole face wrinkles).

### Comparator(s) or control(s)
All study types with a pre-intervention/post-intervention and/or a control group (standard of care or no intervention) and/or alternative method of skin rejuvenation.

### Context
Studies investigating the effectiveness of PN in aesthetic medicine in any country, limited to studies published between 2010–2024.

---

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

### Main outcomes
Influence of polynucleotide on the anatomy and physiology of different skin layers; skin regeneration; hair restoration.

### Measures of effect
- Continuous outcomes: mean difference (MD) — baseline vs end-of-follow-up data for both groups
- Dichotomous outcomes: risk ratio (RR) with 95% CI
- p-value < 0.05 considered statistically significant

### Additional outcomes
- Adverse events
- Changes in patient-reported outcomes (e.g., quality of life)

---

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

### Data extraction (selection and coding)

**Study selection:**
- Two reviewers with academic/research and aesthetics background independently screen
- Reviewers blinded to each other's decisions
- Disagreements resolved through discussion; third reviewer for arbitration
- References imported into Covidence/Rayyan; duplicates excluded
- Titles/abstracts screened against inclusion/exclusion criteria
- Full texts retrieved and assessed for eligibility

**Data extraction:**
- Performed independently by two named authors, then compared
- Pre-defined data extraction template in Covidence
- Data extracted: author(s), year, country, clinical setting, study aim(s), design, sample size, participant characteristics (age, gender, ethnicity, comorbidities, cosmetic indication), treatment area, PN dose, PN administration mode, follow-up duration, adverse events, key results/findings

### Risk of bias (quality) assessment
CASP checklist for each study design. Two authors independently assess quality; disagreements discussed with third author.

---

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

### Strategy for data synthesis
Due to suspected heterogeneity, a **narrative approach** is most likely. Meta-analysis will be performed when at least **three studies** report the same outcome domain of interest.

For outcome measures where meta-analysis is not possible, a narrative data synthesis will be performed in line with Cochrane Handbook guidance. A table of all included studies will be presented with narrative synthesis and critical discussion focusing on similarities, differences, and research gaps.

### Analysis of subgroups or subsets
**No subgroup analysis planned** for this review.
