## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

### Review title
The efficacy of autologous epidermal cell suspensions for re-epithelialization of skin lesion: A systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized trials

### Review objectives
ReCell technology is an epidermal cell transplantation technique originating from Australia. Its principle is to directly process small skin patches into epidermal cell suspensions through enzymatic hydrolysis and physical reactions. As an emerging technology aimed at wound repair, autologous epidermal cell transplantation technology represented by ReCell has been applied to various skin injuries (vitiligo, burns, diabetic foot wounds, pressure ulcers). Due to the small number of studies, its effectiveness and safety have not yet been evaluated by evidence-based medicine. This work aims to systematically review the effects of autologous epidermal cell suspensions for re-epithelialization of skin deficiency.

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

### Condition or domain being studied
Autologous epidermal cell transplantation processes a small area of skin into an epidermal cell suspension through enzymatic hydrolysis. Initially applied to burn wounds, it also contains ~3.5% melanocytes (suitable for vitiligo and scar pigment loss). Now applied to diabetic foot ulcers and pressure ulcers.

### Population
- **Included:** Patients of all ages with skin lesions caused by different reasons who completed the trial in hospital
- **Excluded:** Autologous skin cell suspensions not used to repair skin injury wounds (e.g., autologous dermal cell transplantation alone)

### Intervention(s) or exposure(s)
Trials using placebo, basic treatment, or other techniques for repairing skin wounds as the control group treatment.

### Comparator(s) or control(s)
Only randomized controlled trials (RCTs) will be included.

---

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

### Main outcomes
The major outcome measure will be the **complete epithelialization healing time**.

### Additional outcomes
Secondary outcomes: preparation required area, available area for wound repair, surgical time, complete epithelial healing rate, pain, discoloration, complications, scar score, and skin disease quality of life index.

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

### Data extraction (selection and coding)
For each study fulfilling inclusion criteria, extracted data includes: author, publication year, country; population characteristics (sample size, study design, type of subjects, sex proportion, baseline mean age); characteristics of autologous epidermal cell suspension (type, dosage, duration); and outcomes. All recorded in Microsoft Office Excel.

### Risk of bias (quality) assessment
The Cochrane Collaboration's tool for assessing risk of bias in **Revman 5.4** software will be used:
1. Random sequence generation (selection bias)
2. Allocation concealment (selection bias)
3. Blinding of participants and personnel (performance bias)
4. Blinding of outcome assessment (detection bias)
5. Incomplete outcome data (attrition bias)
6. Selective reporting (reporting bias)
7. Other bias

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

### Strategy for data synthesis
- Discrete numerical variables: risk ratio (RR) with 95% CI
- Continuous outcomes: standardized mean difference (SMD)
- Heterogeneity: I² statistic; forest plots generated and double-checked
- I² < 50%: low heterogeneity → fixed-effects model
- I² > 50%: high heterogeneity → random-effects model
- Subgroup analyses: age, type of intervention, research design, follow-up duration
- Leave-one-out sensitivity analysis
- Publication bias: Egger's regression test (p<0.05 significant)
- Funnel plot asymmetry: "trim and fill" method
- Fail-safe N method to estimate missing studies needed to nullify significance
- **All statistical analyses using StataSE version 15.1**
- p-value < 0.05 considered statistically significant (except for heterogeneity test)

### Analysis of subgroups or subsets
Other indicators affecting heterogeneity: different age groups, research design types, medication times, sample sizes.
