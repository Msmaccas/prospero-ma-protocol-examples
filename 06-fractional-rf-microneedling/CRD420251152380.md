## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

### Review title
Protocol for a Systematic Review of the efficacy and safety of fractional microneedling radiofrequency for facial rejuvenation

### Condition or domain being studied
Radiofrequency microneedling

### Rationale for the review
Facial and neck rejuvenation procedures have gained increasing popularity. Fractional microneedling radiofrequency (RFMN) delivers controlled thermal energy to the dermis while preserving the epidermis, stimulating collagen and elastin remodeling with minimal recovery time.

Fractional microneedling RF combines collagen-inducing benefits of percutaneous microneedles with dermal heating and tightening effects of RF energy. Microneedles create controlled microchannels for targeted, deeper delivery of RF energy, hypothesized to lead to more robust neocollagenesis and elastogenesis compared to non-invasive RF alone.

Clinical studies support efficacy and safety of RF-based aesthetic procedures. Bipolar fractional RF has been shown to induce dermal neocollagenesis and elastogenesis with minimal adverse effects. Significant submental volume reduction, high patient satisfaction, and tolerable discomfort have been demonstrated following fractional RF microneedling for facial and neck rejuvenation.

Despite growing evidence, most studies emphasize quantitative outcomes (wrinkle grading, elasticity measurements, histologic findings). Patient-centered outcomes (perceived effectiveness, satisfaction, long-term acceptability, treatment-related concerns) remain underexplored — these are essential in elective aesthetic procedures.

### Review objectives
To evaluate the efficacy and safety of fractional microneedling radiofrequency for facial and neck rejuvenation in adults, based on its ability to improve skin elasticity and firmness, reduce wrinkles and sagging, increase patient satisfaction, and minimize adverse events, compared to baseline, other aesthetic treatments, or no intervention.

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

### Population
**Included:** Adult patients (≥18 years) who have received fractional microneedling radiofrequency (RFMN) treatment for aesthetic rejuvenation of the face and/or neck. Studies involving mixed anatomical regions eligible only if face/neck outcomes are reported or extractable.

### Intervention(s) or exposure(s)
**Included:**
- Fractional microneedle RF devices where RF energy is delivered via penetrating microneedles
- All studies must involve the device applied to the face and/or neck

**Excluded:**
- Ablative or non-ablative RF devices that do not utilize microneedles (monopolar, bipolar, multipolar RF without microneedling)
- Other non-RF microneedling devices (microneedling alone, microneedling with PRP)
- Non-energy-based interventions

### Comparator(s) or control(s)
- Baseline data from the same patient (within-subject comparison)
- Alternative cosmetic treatments (lasers, ultrasound, microneedling)
- No treatment or placebo
- Studies without comparators still included if providing relevant narrative/qualitative outcome data

### Study design
Both randomized and nonrandomized study types will be included.

### Context
Heterogeneity in RF device types, treatment protocols (energy settings, session frequency), and outcome measures complicates evidence comparison. A **qualitative systematic review** is warranted to synthesize narrative evidence capturing patient perspectives, clinician experiences, and contextual factors.

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

### Data extraction from published articles and reports
Data extracted by one person (or a machine) and checked by at least one other person (or machine). Authors asked to provide any required data not available in published reports.

### Study risk of bias or quality assessment
**Tool used:** JBI (Joanna Briggs Institute)

Data assessed independently by at least two people (or person/machine combination) with a process to resolve differences. Additional information sought from study investigators if required.

### Reporting bias assessment
Two reviewers will independently assess each study across JBI tool domains:
- Congruity between research methodology and research questions
- Representation of participants and context
- Ethical considerations
- Data analysis and interpretation
- Researcher influence
- Reporting clarity

### Certainty assessment
Certainty of findings will **not** be assessed.

### Main outcomes
Qualitative or narrative outcomes including but not limited to:
- Patient or clinician perceptions of effectiveness or satisfaction
- Descriptions of aesthetic improvements (firmness, elasticity)
- Tolerability, pain, or comfort levels described narratively
- Expectations, preferences, and concerns regarding treatment
- Adverse experiences described subjectively

### Additional outcomes
There are no additional outcomes.

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

### Strategy for data synthesis
A **narrative and thematic synthesis** approach will be applied:
- Structured data extracted into standardized Excel spreadsheet (study characteristics, population details, intervention type, comparators, reported outcomes)
- For narrative results: **inductive thematic synthesis**
- Thematic coding using a qualitative analysis tool with collaborative tagging and code management
- Two independent reviewers identify relevant codes and concepts
- Organized into broader analytical themes through discussion and consensus
- Third reviewer consulted for discrepancies
- Emergent themes summarized in a synthesis matrix with structured table, supported by representative quotations
- Subgroup analysis based on RF modality or other study-level characteristics where applicable
- Numerical outcomes (standardized wrinkle scales, satisfaction scores) summarized descriptively
- **No quantitative meta-analysis** will be performed
