## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

### Review title
A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis Evaluating the Use of Exosomes in Aesthetics and Aesthetic Plastic Surgery

### Review objectives
**Primary objective:** To evaluate the effectiveness of exosome-based therapies in facial rejuvenation compared to standard or placebo treatments in human clinical studies.

**Secondary objectives:**
- To assess the impact of exosome therapy on specific facial aging outcomes (wrinkles, pigmentation, skin elasticity, texture)
- To compare the efficacy of different exosome delivery methods (topical, microneedling-assisted, laser-assisted)
- To examine whether exosome source (adipose-derived, platelet-derived, bovine milk-derived) influences outcomes
- To identify and describe any reported adverse effects
- To evaluate methodological quality and outcome measurement consistency across included studies

### Keywords
aesthetics; exosome; Aesthetic surgery; Plastic surgery; Exosomes; Facial rejuvenation

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

### Condition or domain being studied
Aging; Wrinkled Skin; Acne Scar; Skin laxity

Focuses on exosomes in aesthetic medicine for facial rejuvenation — nanosized extracellular vesicles involved in intercellular communication, aiming to improve skin quality by enhancing collagen synthesis, promoting angiogenesis, and reducing signs of aging.

### Population
- **Included:** Adult humans with facial aging
- **Excluded:** Human adolescents

### Intervention(s) or exposure(s)
All treatments must include exosomes. Delivery methods include laser therapy, micro-needle, radiofrequency microneedling, carbon dioxide laser, topical application.

### Comparator(s) or control(s)
Saline; Sham Intervention

### Context
Studies evaluating the effectiveness of exosomes on aesthetic outcomes. Research without measurable outcomes was excluded.

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

### Main outcomes
- **Global Aesthetic Improvement:** Measured by validated tools (GAIS or comparable clinician- or patient-reported outcome measures)
- **Wrinkle reduction:** Changes in wrinkle depth, roughness, or area measured by imaging systems (PRIMOS or VISIA)

### Additional outcomes
- Improvement in skin pigmentation (melanin index, hyperpigmented areas via VISIA or Mark-Vu)
- Improvement in skin elasticity (Cutometer)
- Enhancement in skin texture (pore size, surface smoothness)
- Histological changes (collagen or elastin fiber density)
- Adverse effects (erythema, pain, downtime, serious or unexpected side effects)
- Patient satisfaction (DLQI, MELASQoL, or study-specific questionnaires)

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

### Data extraction (selection and coding)
Two reviewers will independently screen titles/abstracts using predetermined inclusion/exclusion criteria. Full-text articles independently evaluated by both reviewers. Discrepancies resolved through discussion. Screening conducted using **Rayyan AI-Powered Systematic Review Management Platform**.

### Risk of bias (quality) assessment
- **For randomized trials:** Cochrane ROB2 risk of bias tool (randomization process, deviations from intended intervention, missing outcome data, measurement of outcome, selection of reported results)
- **For other studies:** Similar parameters assessed (allocation sequence generation, treatment allocation concealment, blinding, selective outcome reporting, other sources of bias)
- Risk of bias reported as low, some concerns, or high per Cochrane handbook criteria
- Disagreements resolved via discussion

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

### Strategy for data synthesis
- **Dichotomous outcomes:** Risk ratios (RRs) with 95% CI
- **Continuous outcomes:** Mean differences (MDs) with 95% CI
- **Clinical heterogeneity:** Comparison of trial characteristics (population, intervention, outcome measures)
- **Statistical heterogeneity:** Visual inspection of forest plots, Chi-squared (χ²) test, I² statistic
  - I² > 50%: substantial heterogeneity
  - p < 0.10 on χ² test: statistically significant heterogeneity
- **Meta-analysis model:** Fixed-effect model (weighted average method) if homogeneous; random-effects model if substantial heterogeneity detected (with justification)

### Analysis of subgroups or subsets
If sufficient data available:
- **Mode of application:** Topical, microneedling-assisted, or laser-assisted delivery
- **Exosome source:** Adipose-derived, platelet-derived, bovine milk-derived, rose stem-cell-derived
- **Meta-regression:** Random-effects meta-regression to investigate association between treatment effect and study duration or exosome dose (if ≥10 studies)
