**Key observation:** Some timelines are clearly aspirational. A 1.5-month review (from search to data synthesis) for #5 Exosomes is ambitious. The completed reviews (#3 at 11.5 months, #6 at 1 month but already completed by registration date) show that **a realistic timeline for a solo or small-team review is 6–12 months.**

PROSPERO does not hold you to the submitted timeline — you can update it as you go. But an unrealistically short timeline will look naive to journal reviewers.

**For you as a single junior researcher (MBChB student):**
You should list yourself as the guarantor/review contact. Having a supervisor named (even if their role is advisory) strengthens the record. The Lampridou model (#3) is most appropriate — single primary author with a named collaborator.

### 9.3 Funding and COI Declaration

**Funding patterns:**
- Most examples: "Not applicable" or "No funding"
- #4 (Lou): Named specific grants (HwaMei Foundation, Ningbo projects) — transparent
- PROSPERO does not require funding, but declaring "No funding" is better than leaving it blank

**COI patterns:**
- #3 (Lampridou): Named that GC is a KOL and clinical trainer for a PN distributing company (Dermafocus / Mastelli Srl)
- Most others: "None declared"
- Being transparent about COI (even if there are none) shows integrity

### 9.4 Publication Intentions

**Important:** PROSPERO asks whether you intend to publish. The options are:
- "The review is completed/published" (for retrospective updates)
- "The intention is to publish the review once completed"
- "The intention is not to publish the review once completed" (#1 and #4 chose this — this is a flag for journal reviewers who check PROSPERO)

If you PLAN to publish (which you do — J Cosmet Dermatol or Dermatol Surg), say so. Don't select "not to publish" even if you're unsure.

### 9.5 PROSPERO Common Errors — Avoid These

Based on analysis of PROSPERO audit outcomes, here are the most common errors that cause returns or weaken submissions (adapted from Rombey et al., J Clin Epidemiol 2020):

**Critical errors (causes return/rejection):**
1. No funding/COI statement — field #11–12 mandatory; write "No funding received" + "All authors declare no competing interests"
2. No minimum follow-up duration defined in eligibility criteria
3. No language strategy — PROSPERO expects clarity; "Published in English" is operational and honest
4. Incomplete/untested search strings — provide EXACT PubMed string with [MeSH] + [tiab] and test against positive controls
5. No search date range — "From inception to [date]"
6. No pilot extraction described — "Form piloted on 5 studies"
7. Over-promising meta-analysis when data unlikely to permit it — "Narrative synthesis if <3 studies or high heterogeneity"
8. No GRADE — required for intervention SRs
9. Missing RoB tool specification — generic "Cochrane tool" insufficient; name RoB 2 / ROBINS-I
10. No outcome-time matrix — create a table mapping each outcome to primary/secondary timepoints

**Moderate errors (weaken protocol):**
11. Third reviewer not secured — state "disagreements resolved by discussion; senior author makes final determination"
12. Vague database descriptions — name each database and justify selection
13. Single-arm and comparative studies not separated in analysis
14. No publication bias plan
15. No sensitivity analysis plan
16. Conference abstract policy not addressed
17. No data extraction items list
