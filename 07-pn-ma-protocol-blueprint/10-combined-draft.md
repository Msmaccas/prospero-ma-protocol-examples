## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

### Review title
Polynucleotides for Skin Rejuvenation: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Randomized and Split-Face Controlled Trials

### Rationale
Polynucleotide (PN) preparations — injectable formulations derived from salmon or human DNA fragments — have emerged as a widely used biostimulatory treatment for facial skin rejuvenation. Marketed for improving skin texture, elasticity, hydration, and wrinkle reduction, PN injections are commonly used in Asia and increasingly adopted in Europe and North America. The proposed mechanism involves fibroblast stimulation, increased collagen and elastin synthesis, and enhanced extracellular matrix hydration via nucleotide-mediated signalling pathways.

Two systematic reviews have recently synthesised the PN literature. Lampridou et al. (2025, *J Cosmet Dermatol*) included 9 studies (219 patients) across aesthetic PN applications and performed a narrative synthesis, concluding that evidence quality was low-to-moderate and precluded meta-analysis. Kream et al. (2026, *Dermatol Surg*) provided a broader overview of PN and PDRN across all dermatologic applications without performing a quantitative pooled analysis.

Since the publication of these reviews, additional controlled trials have been published, and sufficient data have accumulated across multiple split-face and parallel-group RCTs using validated outcome measures (GAIS, Cutometer, VISIA) to support a quantitative synthesis. A meta-analysis of PN efficacy for skin rejuvenation — specifically, the pooled effect size across comparable outcomes — has not been performed. The present review aims to address this gap.

### Review objectives
**Primary objective:** To evaluate the efficacy of injectable polynucleotide (PN) and polydeoxyribonucleotide (PDRN) therapies for facial skin rejuvenation in adults, compared to placebo, no treatment, hyaluronic acid fillers, or other active comparators, as measured by validated clinician- and patient-reported outcome instruments.

**Secondary objectives:**
1. To assess the impact of PN therapy on individual skin aging domains: wrinkle reduction (depth, area, severity), skin elasticity, hydration, texture, and pigmentation
2. To compare efficacy across PN formulations (PN-HPT, standard PN, PDRN) and delivery methods (injection alone vs. with microneedling or laser assistance)
3. To evaluate the safety profile of PN therapy by synthesising adverse event data
4. To assess the methodological quality of included studies and consistency of outcome measurement across trials

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

### Condition or domain being studied
Facial skin aging — including wrinkles (fine lines and deep rhytides), loss of skin elasticity, reduced skin hydration, textural irregularities, and pigmentation changes associated with chronological aging and photoaging. Studies of PN for aesthetic purposes in non-facial sites (neck, décolletage, hands) will be included if rejuvenation outcomes are separately reported.

### Population
**Included:** Adult participants (≥18 years) of any sex, ethnicity, or Fitzpatrick skin type receiving injectable polynucleotide therapy for facial rejuvenation.

**Excluded:**
- Participants <18 years
- Animal studies, in vitro studies, or ex vivo studies
- Patients receiving PN for non-rejuvenation indications exclusively (acne scars, surgical wounds, alopecia) without separately reported rejuvenation outcomes
- Studies limited to perioral rejuvenation only

### Intervention(s) or exposure(s)
**Included:** Injectable polynucleotide (PN), highly purified polynucleotide (PN-HPT), or polydeoxyribonucleotide (PDRN) preparations administered via intradermal, subcutaneous, or microinjection techniques. All formulations, concentrations, doses, treatment intervals, and session numbers are eligible. Studies combining PN with microneedling, laser pretreatment, or topical delivery are eligible if the PN-specific effect can be isolated.

**Excluded:** Oral or topical PN formulations; non-PN biostimulators (PLLA, CaHA, PCL) unless used as a comparator; PRP or exosome therapies.

### Comparator(s) or control(s)
Eligible comparators include: placebo (saline or vehicle injection), no treatment (untreated control side in split-face designs), hyaluronic acid fillers, alternative biostimulatory agents, or baseline (pre-treatment) within-patient measurements. Studies without a comparator arm will be excluded from efficacy analyses but included for safety characterisation.

### Study design
**Included:**
- Randomized controlled trials (parallel-group design)
- Split-face or split-body randomized controlled trials (within-patient design)
- Prospective non-randomized controlled trials with concurrent control groups

**Excluded:**
- Case reports and case series (n < 5)
- Retrospective studies without a concurrent control group
- Narrative reviews, systematic reviews, meta-analyses, editorials, conference abstracts
- Cross-sectional studies

### Context
No geographic restrictions. Studies from any country or healthcare setting will be eligible. Clinic-based, hospital-based, and medical spa settings are all eligible.

### Search for unpublished studies
Only published studies will be sought through the databases and trial registries listed above. Grey literature will not be systematically searched, but relevant studies cited by included papers or existing reviews will be considered.

### Search language restrictions
The review will only include studies published in English. Studies published in other languages that are identified through the search will be documented and assessed for translation feasibility.

### Search date restrictions
Databases will be searched for articles published from 1 January 2015 to the date of the search.

### Other methods of identifying studies
Other studies will be identified by: (1) checking reference lists of all included studies and of existing systematic reviews (Lampridou 2025, Kream 2026), and (2) citation tracking of key included studies.

### Link to search strategy
A full search strategy for each database will be uploaded to PROSPERO as a supplementary file.

### Selection process
Titles and abstracts of all retrieved records will be independently screened by two reviewers against the predetermined eligibility criteria. If dual independent screening is not feasible, a single reviewer will conduct two independent screening rounds using separate user accounts in the review management platform, separated by a minimum of 7 days.

Full texts of potentially eligible studies will be retrieved and independently assessed. Reasons for exclusion at the full-text stage will be documented and reported in the PRISMA 2020 flow diagram. Discrepancies will be resolved through discussion; a third reviewer (supervisor) will be consulted if necessary.

Screening will be managed using Covidence or Rayyan.

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

### Main outcomes
**Primary outcome:** Global Aesthetic Improvement — measured by the Global Aesthetic Improvement Scale (GAIS) or equivalent clinician- or patient-reported global improvement instrument at 3 months post-treatment (or the nearest available time point). Where GAIS is reported as a categorical variable (improved vs not improved), risk ratios will be calculated. Where GAIS is reported as a continuous variable (mean ± SD), standardised mean differences will be used.

### Additional outcomes
**Secondary outcomes:**
1. Wrinkle severity — measured by WSRS or validated wrinkle grading scales, or 3D imaging (PRIMOS, VISIA). SMD at 3 months.
2. Skin elasticity — measured by Cutometer parameters (R2 gross elasticity or equivalent). SMD at 3 months.
3. Skin hydration — measured by Corneometer capacitance values. MD or SMD at 3 months.
4. Patient satisfaction — measured by patient-reported satisfaction scales or VAS. SMD at 3 months.
5. Safety — incidence of adverse events (injection site reactions, bruising, swelling, pain, nodule formation). Risk ratios or narrative summary.

**Time points:** Primary time point: 3 months (range: 8–16 weeks). Sensitivity time points: 1 month (≤6 weeks) and 6 months (≥20 weeks). If multiple time points are reported, the time point closest to 3 months will be used for the primary analysis.

**Handling multiple instruments:** Where different instruments measure the same construct, SMD with Hedges' g correction will be used. The direction of effect will be standardised so that higher values represent improvement.

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

### Data extraction (selection and coding)
Data will be extracted independently by two reviewers using a pre-piloted data extraction form in Covidence/Microsoft Excel.

**Extracted data fields:**
- Study information: author(s), year, journal, country, study design, sample size, funding
- Participant characteristics: age, sex, Fitzpatrick skin type, baseline condition, prior treatment washout
- Intervention details: PN formulation and concentration, volume per session, number of sessions, intervals between sessions, injection technique, delivery method, treatment area(s)
- Comparator details: type (placebo/HA/other), for split-face: which side received which treatment
- Outcome data: for each outcome at each time point — mean, SD, and n per group (parallel) OR mean difference and SD of difference (split-face); event counts and n for dichotomous outcomes
- Adverse events: number, type, and severity per group

**For split-face studies:** Paired data (mean difference and SD of the difference) will be extracted where reported. Where only pre- and post-treatment means and SDs per hemiface are reported, the SD of the within-patient difference will be imputed using a correlation coefficient of r = 0.5. Sensitivity analyses will use r = 0.25 and r = 0.75.

**Missing data:** Corresponding authors will be contacted by email (maximum 2 attempts, 2 weeks apart) for unreported outcome data. If data remain unavailable, the study will be included in narrative synthesis but excluded from the relevant meta-analysis.

### Risk of bias (quality) assessment
The revised Cochrane Risk of Bias tool for randomized trials (RoB 2) will be used for all included RCTs, including split-face designs. For split-face trials:
- Domain 1: Assessment will focus on hemiface-level randomisation and allocation concealment
- Domain 4: Assessor blinding (clinician, patient, independent rater) will be of particular importance
- The standard RoB 2 tool will be applied with these domain-specific considerations

For non-randomised controlled studies, the ROBINS-I tool will be used.

Two reviewers will independently assess risk of bias. Disagreements will be resolved through discussion. Results will be presented as domain-level and overall judgments (low risk / some concerns / high risk).

**Reporting bias:** Funnel plot asymmetry and Egger's regression test (if ≥10 studies). If bias is detected, the trim-and-fill method will be used. Fail-safe N will be reported.

**Certainty of evidence (GRADE):** The GRADE approach will be used to rate certainty for each outcome as high, moderate, low, or very low.

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

### Strategy for data synthesis
**Narrative synthesis:** A narrative synthesis of all included studies will be conducted regardless of meta-analysis feasibility. Studies will be tabulated by design (parallel-group RCT vs. split-face RCT vs. non-randomised controlled trial).

**Quantitative synthesis (meta-analysis):** Will be performed when ≥2 studies report the same outcome construct at comparable time points using combinable data.

**Effect measures:**
- Continuous outcomes: SMD with Hedges' g (different instruments); MD (same instrument/unit)
- Dichotomous outcomes: risk ratio (RR) with 95% CI
- Split-face studies: within-patient MD and SD of difference

**Meta-analysis model:** Primary approach: random-effects model (DerSimonian-Laird). Fixed-effects model reported as sensitivity analysis.

**Heterogeneity:**
- I² statistic: low (25%), moderate (50%), high (75%)
- Chi-squared test: p<0.10 significant
- If I² > 50%: explore via subgroup analyses and meta-regression (if ≥10 studies)

**Subgroup analyses (pre-specified):**
1. PN formulation: PN-HPT vs. standard PN vs. PDRN
2. Treatment area: periorbital vs. pan-facial vs. neck
3. Comparator type: placebo/saline vs. active vs. baseline
4. Study design: parallel RCT vs. split-face RCT vs. non-randomised

**Sensitivity analyses:**
1. Leave-one-out analysis
2. Restricting to low risk of bias studies
3. Fixed-effects model
4. Varying within-patient correlation (r = 0.25, 0.5, 0.75) for split-face data
5. Trim-and-fill adjusted estimates

**Software:** R version 4.x (meta and metafor packages). RevMan 5.4 for forest plots. All code will be made available as supplementary material.
