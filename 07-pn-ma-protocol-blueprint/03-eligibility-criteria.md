## PART I: LEARNINGS & BEST PRACTICES

### 3.1 What Eligibility Criteria Should Cover

PROSPERO asks for eligibility criteria broken into: condition/domain, population, intervention(s)/exposure(s), comparator(s)/control(s), study design, and context.

**From the 6 examples, the strongest eligibility sections:**

**#2 Collagen Biostimulators** — uses Included/Excluded subheadings for each criterion:
> Population — Included: "Healthy adults (≥18 years) receiving collagen biostimulator injections for facial aesthetics" / Excluded: "Participants <18 years, non-human studies, or treatments for non-aesthetic purposes"

**#6 RF Microneedling** — uses detailed intervention inclusion/exclusion:
> Included: "Fractional microneedle RF devices where RF energy is delivered via penetrating microneedles"
> Excluded: "Ablative or non-ablative RF devices that do not utilize microneedles... Other non-RF microneedling devices... Non-energy-based interventions"

**#1 BTX-A+HA** — structures eligibility as a search strategy narrative:
> "Inclusion Criteria: Adults undergoing facial rejuvenation treatments using botulinum toxin type A and/or hyaluronic acid..."

### 3.2 The PICOS Framework — Details That Matter

**Population considerations for aesthetic derm SRs:**
- Age minimum (≥18 years is standard)
- Health status (healthy volunteers vs. patients with specific conditions)
- Fitzpatrick skin types (relevant if safety differs by skin type)
- Prior treatment washout periods (the Lampridou review excluded patients with prior treatments within 6 months — is this appropriate for your review?)

**Intervention specificity — the goldilocks problem:**
- **Too broad:** "Any PN treatment" — captures heterogeneous studies, hard to pool
- **Too narrow:** "PN-HPT 2mL injected every 4 weeks for 3 sessions" — excludes most studies
- **Just right:** "Injectable PN, PN-HPT, or PDRN for facial aesthetic purposes, any dose or regimen" (#2 approach)

**Comparator — the critical decision point for your review:**
Your review faces a specific challenge: split-face studies often compare PN to:
- Saline/placebo (blinded split-face)
- No treatment (untreated side)
- HA filler (active comparator)
- Baseline (pre-treatment within the same patient)

Parallel-group studies compare PN to:
- Placebo
- Other active treatments
- No treatment

**How the examples handle multiple comparator types:**
- #4 (Lou): Only RCTs, control = "placebo, basic treatment or other techniques" — vague but works for MA
- #5 (Stack): "Saline; Sham Intervention" — clean binary
- #6 (Kumar): Broad range including "baseline data from the same patient" — appropriate for qualitative review

**Recommendation for you:** Accept all comparator types but document how each will be analysed (separate subgroups or combined with appropriate adjustment).

### 3.3 Study Design Decisions

**What each example includes:**

| Example | RCTs | Non-RCTs | Split-face | Case series | Observational |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| #1 BTX-A+HA | ✓ | ✓ | Not specified | Not specified | ✓ |
| #2 Collagen | ✓ | ✓ | Not specified | Not specified | ✓ |
| #3 PN (Lampridou) | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ (implied) | ✗ | ✓ |
| #4 ReCell | Only RCTs | ✗ | N/A | ✗ | ✗ |
| #5 Exosomes | ✓ | ✓ | Not specified | ✗ | ✗ |
| #6 RFMN | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✗ | ✗ |

**Key observation:** The strongest reviews (#4 and #5) explicitly state what they EXCLUDE, not just what they include. Many weak protocols list only inclusion criteria.

**For split-face designs — a specific note:**
Split-face trials are NOT the same as parallel-group RCTs. They need their own handling:
- The unit of randomisation is the hemiface, not the patient
- Outcomes are paired within patients
- Traditional meta-analysis methods for parallel trials assume independence between groups — this is violated in split-face data if not handled correctly

### 3.4 Language and Date Restrictions

**Language:**
- 4 of 6 examples restricted to English
- #3 (Lampridou) specifically said "No language restrictions" — this is Cochrane-recommended best practice BUT resource-intensive
- #1 did not specify language (weakness)

**Date range:**
- 2010–2024 (PN #3)
- 2000–2025 (Collagen #2)
- 2015–2025 (RFMN #6 — this is very narrow, appropriate for a newer technology)
- Not specified (#1, #4 — notable weakness)

**For your PN review:** PN research accelerated around 2018–2020. A 2015–2026 date range captures the relevant period. Going back to 2000 might catch early PDRN studies but they may have different formulations and outcome measures.

### 3.5 PROSPERO-Specific Considerations

PROSPERO does not have a mandatory structured "PICOS" field — it asks for Population, Intervention, Comparator, and Study Design as separate free-text fields. The "Context" field is optional but useful for geographic/healthcare-setting restrictions.

## PART II: YOUR PROTOCOL DRAFT

### Draft Eligibility Criteria

> **Condition or domain being studied:**
> Facial skin aging — including wrinkles (fine lines and deep rhytides), loss of skin elasticity, reduced skin hydration, textural irregularities, and pigmentation changes associated with chronological aging and photoaging. Studies of PN for aesthetic purposes in non-facial sites (neck, décolletage, hands) will be included if rejuvenation outcomes are separately reported.
>
> **Population — Included:**
> Adult participants (≥18 years) of any sex, ethnicity, or Fitzpatrick skin type receiving injectable polynucleotide therapy for facial rejuvenation.
>
> **Population — Excluded:**
> - Participants <18 years
> - Animal studies, in vitro studies, or ex vivo studies
> - Patients receiving PN for non-rejuvenation indications exclusively (acne scars, surgical wounds, alopecia) without separately reported rejuvenation outcomes
> - Studies limited to perioral rejuvenation only (lip enhancement) without facial skin outcomes
>
> **Intervention — Included:**
> Injectable polynucleotide (PN), highly purified polynucleotide (PN-HPT), or polydeoxyribonucleotide (PDRN) preparations administered via intradermal, subcutaneous, or microinjection techniques. All formulations, concentrations, doses, treatment intervals, and session numbers are eligible. Studies combining PN with microneedling, laser pretreatment, or topical delivery are eligible if the PN-specific effect can be isolated.
>
> **Intervention — Excluded:**
> - Oral or topical PN formulations (not injectable)
> - Non-PN biostimulators (PLLA, CaHA, PCL — unless used as a comparator)
> - Platelet-rich plasma (PRP) or exosome therapies
>
> **Comparator:**
> Eligible comparators include: placebo (saline or vehicle injection), no treatment (untreated control side in split-face designs), hyaluronic acid fillers, alternative biostimulatory agents, or baseline (pre-treatment) within-patient measurements. Studies without a comparator arm will be excluded from efficacy analyses but included for safety characterisation.
>
> **Study design — Included:**
> - Randomized controlled trials (parallel-group design)
> - Split-face or split-body randomized controlled trials (within-patient design)
> - Prospective non-randomized controlled trials with concurrent control groups
>
> **Study design — Excluded:**
> - Case reports and case series (n < 5)
> - Retrospective studies without a concurrent control group
> - Narrative reviews, systematic reviews, meta-analyses, editorials, conference abstracts
> - Cross-sectional studies
>
> **Context:**
> No geographic restrictions. Studies from any country or healthcare setting will be eligible. Clinic-based, hospital-based, and medical spa settings are all eligible.
>
> **Language:**
> Studies published in English will be included. No restriction on studies in other languages — if identified, they will be documented and assessed for translation feasibility.
>
> **Date range:**
> 1 January 2015 to present (anticipated search date). This captures the period of modern PN formulation use and validated outcome instrument application.
